How to Crochet Nylon Net Scrubbies

Now that you're excited to start your crochet journey with nylon net scrubbies, let's go over how to make them. Here's a step-by-step guide to get you started:

Materials Needed

  1. Nylon netting in your desired colors
  2. Crochet hook (recommended size G)
  3. Scissors

Step 1: Preparing the Netting

Begin by cutting the nylon netting into strips. The width of the strips will determine the size of your scrubbies. You can cut them into 1-inch strips for smaller scrubbies and 2-inch strips for larger ones.

Step 2: Creating the Foundation Chain

Start by making a slipknot with one of the netting strips. Then, chain 4 stitches. Insert your hook into the first chain stitch, forming a circle. This will serve as the foundation of your scrubbie.

Step 3: Single Crochet

Next, work single crochet stitches into the center of the circle you've created. Keep working single crochets until you have a tight cluster of stitches, forming a small circle.

Step 4: Increasing the Stitches

To increase the size of your scrubbie, you'll need to increase the number of stitches in each round. To do this, work two single crochet stitches into each stitch of the previous round. Continue this pattern until you reach your desired size.

Benefits and Advantages

Crocheting nylon net scrubbies comes with several benefits and advantages. Let's take a look at them:

  • Durable: Nylon netting is known for its durability, ensuring that your scrubbies will last a long time.
  • Effective Cleaning: These scrubbies are excellent for cleaning dishes, pots, and pans due to their rough texture.
  • Reusable: Unlike disposable sponges, nylon net scrubbies can be easily washed and reused, making them eco-friendly options.
  • Customizable: You can choose various colors and patterns of netting to create unique and personalized scrubbies.

People Also Ask

Q: How do I clean nylon net scrubbies?

A: To clean nylon net scrubbies, simply wash them with soap and warm water after each use. You can also toss them in the washing machine with your regular laundry.

Q: Can I use cotton yarn instead of nylon netting?

A: Yes, you can use cotton yarn to crochet scrubbies. However, keep in mind that nylon netting provides a rougher texture for more effective scrubbing.

Q: Are nylon net scrubbies safe for non-stick pans?

A: Nylon net scrubbies are generally safe for non-stick pans. However, it's always recommended to test a small area first to ensure they don't scratch the surface.
