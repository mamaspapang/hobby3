How to Crochet One Round Fall Leaves

Before we begin, make sure you have the necessary materials on hand. You'll need a crochet hook, yarn in your desired colors, and a pair of scissors. Once you have everything ready, follow these steps:

  1. Step 1: Start with a Magic Ring

    Begin by making a magic ring. If you're not familiar with this technique, don't worry, it's quite simple. Hold the end of your yarn with your thumb and forefinger, leaving a tail. Wrap the yarn around your fingers to create a loop, then insert your hook through the loop. Yarn over and pull through, creating a chain stitch. This forms the center of your magic ring.

  2. Step 2: Crochet the First Round

    Now that you have your magic ring, it's time to crochet the first round of stitches. Insert your hook into the magic ring, yarn over, and pull through. This creates your first chain stitch. Repeat this process until you have a total of number chain stitches, depending on the desired size of your leaf.

    Once you have the desired number of chain stitches, slip stitch into the first chain stitch to join the round. This forms a loop that you will work into for the next steps.

  3. Step 3: Create the Leaf Shape

    To create the leaf shape, you'll be working a combination of stitches into the loop from the previous round. Begin by working a single crochet stitch into the loop, followed by a half-double crochet stitch, and then a double crochet stitch. Repeat this pattern until you reach the end of the round.

    Once you've completed the last stitch, slip stitch into the first single crochet stitch to join the round. This forms the base of your leaf shape.
