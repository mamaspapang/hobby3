Hey friend! Today I want to share with you a quick and super easy way to join new yarn in crochet in 2021. Crocheting is such a fun and creative hobby, and learning how to properly join yarn is an important skill to have. So, let's dive right in and get started!

When it comes to joining new yarn in crochet, there are several methods you can choose from. One popular method is called the "magic knot." This technique involves tying a knot between the old yarn and the new yarn, creating a secure and seamless join. It's a great option when you're working with different colors or want to avoid weaving in ends later.

Using the Magic Knot Technique

To join new yarn using the magic knot technique, follow these simple steps:

Step 1: Leave a Tail

Start by leaving a tail of about 6 inches from both the old yarn and the new yarn. This will give you enough yarn to weave in the ends later.

Step 2: Create a Loop

Hold the ends of both yarns together, forming a loop with the old yarn on the left and the new yarn on the right.

Step 3: Tie a Knot

Take the end of the new yarn and pass it through the loop from top to bottom. Then, bring it around and pass it through the loop again, this time from bottom to top. Pull both ends to tighten the knot.

Step 4: Repeat the Knot

Repeat the knot one more time to ensure a secure join. Pass the end of the new yarn through the loop from top to bottom, then bring it around and pass it through the loop from bottom to top. Pull both ends to tighten the knot.

Step 5: Trim Excess Yarn

Once you've tied the knot, trim the excess yarn tails close to the knot. Be careful not to cut too close, as you don't want the knot to come undone.

Now that you know how to join new yarn using the magic knot technique, let's talk about the benefits and advantages of using this method.

Benefits and Advantages

1. Seamless Join: The magic knot technique creates a seamless join between the old yarn and the new yarn, resulting in a neat and professional finish to your crochet project.

2. No Need for Weaving in Ends: By joining the yarn using the magic knot, you eliminate the need for weaving in ends later. This saves you time and effort, especially when working with multiple colors.

3. Strong and Secure: The magic knot creates a strong and secure join that won't come undone with use or washing. It ensures that your crocheted piece stays intact and durable over time.

Now, let's wrap things up with a quick recap.

In Conclusion

Joining new yarn in crochet doesn't have to be complicated. By using the magic knot technique, you can easily and securely join your yarn, creating a seamless finish without the hassle of weaving in ends later. It's a quick and efficient method that every crocheter should know.

Remember, practice makes perfect, so don't be afraid to give it a try on your next crochet project. Happy crocheting, my friend!

People Also Ask

Q: Can I use a different method to join yarn?

A: Absolutely! The magic knot is just one of many methods you can use to join yarn in crochet. Other popular techniques include the slip stitch join, the standing stitch join, and the Russian join. Feel free to explore and find the method that works best for you!

Q: Will the magic knot show through my crocheted project?

A: In most cases, the magic knot blends in seamlessly with your crocheted stitches and won't be visible in the finished project. However, if you're working with a very fine yarn or a lacy stitch pattern, you may want to choose a different joining method to ensure a smoother appearance.
